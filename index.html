FIP (Level 2) Brand Refresh Assignment

This assignment requires students to work as partners, in order to take a defunct brand and give it a complete refresh, creating a new identity and a new product, and then create a responsive website for the brand, while including dynamic features with the use of Javascript.

The first thing is to gather all the details of the task, and start coming up with ideas for the structure of pages and what kind of features and assets the site will have. The site is for a specific brand, and that brand identity needs to be created before being able to start the wireframing. The initial brand name and product is given, but every other aspect of brand identity, such as logos, colour scheme, font choices, and even the specific product design and appearance are all created by the students. This tests creativity, as well as the ability to take creative ideas and have them work well with the site and its design.

Next Low Fidelity wireframes are created, in order to get a general idea of layout, as well as what will be included in the site

Next, comes the high fidelity wireframe, where details become more fleshed out, and the real assets are created, such as logos, images, videos, and the text that will be included in the site. Additionally, colour choices are picked out, in accordance to the brand identity. This part is very important as the final website design will be taken from the high-fidelity wireframe.

The finished high-fidelity wireframe is complete, and now prototyping begins, where functionality such as links, navigation, and dynamic features are implemented. The challenge is to create something that works well, but also ensure that it can be done not just in the wireframe, but in the Javascript as well.

After all of this, development can begin, and the hi-fidelity wireframe comes to life with the use of HTML and CSS, the site is made with its mobile page first, and other versions of the page are then created and transition seamlessly using media queries to create responsive design. In development, the website goes from the static design of the wireframes, to a smooth and responsive site that changes along with the size of the window it appears in.

After the page is created, JavaScript is used to bring the dynamic features onto the site, and they are designed to work as they do in the prototype.

Now the website is complete, with responsive design, dynamic features, and an interesting design.

LEVEL 4 CLIENT WORK


In level four, student groups partner with non-profit organizations focused on bipolar disorder to apply their expertise. These organizations act as clients, and the students will utilize their acquired skills to develop a final website tailored to the specific needs of individuals affected by bipolar disorder.
